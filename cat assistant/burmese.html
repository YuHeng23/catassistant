Burmese

Description

Burmese cats are medium-sized cats with rounded heads, expressive gold eyes, and powerful, muscular bodies. Although this breed is susceptible to a hereditary bone condition, a quick DNA test may rule it out, so you can buy or adopt one knowing that it will be healthy. A Burmese is a pleasant addition to almost any family and is likely to get along with everyone, especially friendly dogs. These cats need a lot of protein and nutrients to support their active lifestyle. They can be as much as 6.4 kilogrammes.

Behavior

The Burmese cat is a unique creature, brimming with energy and mischievous whims. Even as an adult, it still has a kitten-like disposition, and others have said that it is almost dog-like in how devoted it is to its human family. The Burmese cat is probably following its owner around or snuggling for a snooze when it isn't having fun. Males typically supervise from the comfort of a lap, while females are more likely to be actively in charge of a household. Both sexes have pleasant, gentle voices and take pleasure in "conversing" with others.

Lifespan

Up to 18 years

Characteristics

The cat's head should be generally rounded, with full eyes that are rounded and evenly spaced. Additionally, there should be a clear nose break and evenly separated ears. The ideal body type has proportional, muscular, compact legs, and rounded paws. The tail should be medium in length and straight.
